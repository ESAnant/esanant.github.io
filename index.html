About Me

My journey into electronics began with an insatiable curiosity about how things work. As a teenager, I would dismantle everything from TV remotes to old computers, driven by a need to understand the electronic systems that surrounded me.

This hands-on experimentation evolved into formal education and professional expertise. From tinkering with remote-control cars to designing VLSI circuits at 45nm technology, each step has deepened my understanding of semiconductor physics and digital design.

Today, I combine theoretical knowledge with practical innovation, specializing in memory technologies, VLSI design, and embedded systems. My work focuses on bridging the gap between cutting-edge research and real-world applications.

"Technology development is not only about honing technical skills, but also about fostering collaboration and innovation."
