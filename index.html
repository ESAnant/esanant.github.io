01. About Me

My journey into electronics wasn't just a choice; it was a calling. It began with taking apart old gadgets and evolved into a deep academic and professional pursuit, culminating in an M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from the National University of Singapore.

My master's program honed my skills in advanced VLSI design, memory technologies, and hardware acceleration for neural networks. I've moved beyond theory, with hands-on experience designing and verifying components like AHB-to-APB bridges and optimizing interconnects at the 45nm node. I thrive on the challenge of bridging the gap between abstract algorithms and tangible, high-performance hardware.

I am driven to contribute to the next generation of computing systems by designing smarter, faster, and more efficient integrated circuits. I am currently seeking opportunities where I can apply my expertise to solve real-world problems in the semiconductor industry.

02. Featured Projects

Featured Project

FPGA Neural Network Accelerator

Developed and deployed a hardware accelerator for MLP neural network inference on a Xilinx Zynq-7000 FPGA. Achieved a 4x performance increase over a software-only baseline by implementing optimized pipelining and loop unrolling strategies in Verilog and HLS.

  • Verilog
  • HLS
  • Xilinx Vivado
  • Pipelining

Featured Project

In-Memory Compute Circuit Design

Designed and simulated novel in-memory computing circuits aimed at accelerating neural network operations. Utilized NeuroSim and PyTorch for modeling and analysis, focusing on quantization optimization to enhance efficiency.

  • Circuit Design
  • NeuroSim
  • PyTorch
  • MuMax3
